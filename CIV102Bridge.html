Bridge design and analysis

A bridge design project where we had to use engineering knowledge and our engineering jugment to design and build a bridge.

Open final report (PDF)
Project summary

A strong case study for rigorous engineering reasoning

The project consisted on designing and building a bridge that would support weight. We were given a base design that we had to iterate on to achieve maximum weight/strength ratio. We had to use knowledge and skills learned in class about not only construction and handling of materials, but also technical analysis, beam structural analysis, and everything that derives from there.

At first we calculated all the parameters and the maximum strength of design 0, which is the design givem by the teaching team. Around this design we developed a python code, that allowed the user to introduce parameters such as number of side panels, or height of the beam, and the program would run simulations of the train moving along the track and would caclulate the maximum amount of weight that would be supported. Using this code we were able to perform multiple simulations and therefore arrive at the best design for us to build.

When we had the final design chosen, we started with the building process. As the material being used was just a thick piece of paper, we had to plan the cutting very carefully. When we had everything prepared we began the cutting. The individual pieces of paper were then taken and assembled together using the provided glue. Safety measures where used in the process of building the bridge. When the parts were assembled together the bridge was left to dry for 24 hours before the testing. Weight was placed on the bridge, to ensure that the construction is secure.

Position statement reflection

How this project contributed to my position on engineering design

Planning and diligence

The project required careful planning and execution as there was only one chance at the testing part. We had to ensure that all our calculations were correct, that the construction quality was good and that all conditions were accounted for. This connects to my statements about the importance of thorough preparation and validation in engineering design.
